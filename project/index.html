What is Bioinformatics?

Bioinformatics is an interdisciplinary field that combines biology, computer science, and mathematics to analyze and interpret biological data. It plays a crucial role in genomics, drug development, and understanding genetic diseases.

Applications

  • Gene sequencing and annotation
  • Protein structure prediction
  • Evolutionary biology and phylogenetics
  • Precision medicine and diagnostics
